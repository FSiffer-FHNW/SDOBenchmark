The dataset comes with 11'146 training samples and 1'710 test samples.


Evaluation metric

We suggest using the L1-norm for this dataset.
As a rule of thumb, at 90% accuracy you start to be on par with current research results.


Hints and tipps about the data

The data has gaps, but it's reasonably stable

  • The samples' label peak_flux diverges very little from the manual classification of experts (see the analysis)
  • The data went through plenty of verification in code (e.g. here or here)

The data is not stationary

  • There exists a nearly periodic 11-year solar activity cycle.
  • Several detectors experience a decay in functionality over the years in operation. Their images get darker and darker over time. (See e.g. this GitHub issues discussion)

The data is imbalanced

..but the L1-norm takes care of that for you in this case (the FAQ explains why).
The data analyis gives a rough idea of the imbalance in terms of the flux labels "free" (1e-9) with 3586 samples, "B" (> 1e-7) with 2173 samples, "C" (> 1e-6) with 4783 samples, "M" (> 1e-5) with 574 samples, and "X" (> 1e-4) with 30 samples.
