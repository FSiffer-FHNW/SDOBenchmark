  • SDO is the mission and the satellite which is orbiting around earth
  • AIA and HMI are two detectors ("cameras") that record what happens on the sun
  • A single sample is a collection of images over four time steps (Convolutions and RNNs)
  • Not many samples
In September 2017 a flare erupted of a magnitude larger than any other in the last decade. In the dataset, this flare is represented with a peak flux of 0.001 (i.e. 1e-3). The smallest flare that is part of the data is listed with a peak flux of 1e-7 (while 1e-9 is "nothing happens at all").

The easy answer is that we can't artificially produce or simulate more flares. But there is also a strategic choice behind it:
Imagine you have to train a network to recognize cats. We'd give you a training image set of 10 million images, recorded by our cameras around our neighbourhood. While 10 million images is a great size for deep learning, you'd soon come to realize that all our images are from the same 1'000 cats. As a consequence, your model would overfit horribly.
We therefore chose to preselect only a few images per cat, but each as different to the others as possible.
